I study large language models in the pipelines that produce official travel statistics — the data behind mode-share figures, demand models, and infrastructure investment cases. My research documents a failure mode with real policy stakes: LLMs will "repair" data to satisfy whatever an automated verifier asserts, even when the verifier itself is wrong, and the result carries a verified label it did not earn.

The constructive half of the work builds verifiers that are provably sound under the measurement uncertainty of real mobility data, so repair and generation loops can only be asked to fix what the data can actually prove broken. Five connected studies carry this arc from measurement to guarantee, and my dissertation extends it from repairing observed travel diaries to generating certified daily activity schedules.

An Evidence-Based Review of AI-Enhanced Activity-Based Models: Trade-offs Between Interpretability, Fidelity, and Efficiency

Kervin Joshua Lucas, Inhi Kim

Int. J. of Urban Sciences · revision under review

42 scored studies, one map: AI travel models cluster at the fidelity vertex of an interpretability–fidelity–efficiency trilemma; the other two axes go chronically unmeasured.

preprint · scoring data

Abstract & key numbers

Progress in AI-enhanced activity-based travel models is constrained by an under-articulated three-way trade-off among behavioral interpretability, predictive fidelity, and computational efficiency. From a structured review (961 records screened to 42 core studies, 2001–2025), every study is scored on falsifiable indicators and mapped into a normalized trilemma space (I + F + E = 20) with a strict-Pareto frontier. Quality is use-case-dependent: progress reads as movement within target regions, not toward a universal optimum.

Garbage In, Verified Out When the Verifier Is Wrong

Authors withheld during double-blind review

AAAI-27 · under review

Zero refusals in 10,090 encounters: six LLMs, frontier to local 7B, executed repairs against constraints a defective verifier invented — and stronger models were no more skeptical.

response corpus · classification instrument

Abstract & key numbers

LLM-Modulo repair frameworks act on whatever their symbolic verifier asserts. We document a working pipeline in which the verifier was wrong: a data-loading defect made its speed checks assert violations the data could not support. Spontaneous doubt about the data appeared in 1.33% of encounters, and doubt naming the defective value did not track model capability — the frontier model named it once in 1,688 encounters against eighteen times for a local 9B model. Repairs against the unsupported flags collapsed walking among affected travelers from roughly seven in ten trips to under one in ten. Deployed repair loops require verifier verification: the critic's soundness must be audited with the same rigor as the generator's outputs.

Sound Verifiers for LLM-Modulo Data Repair under Measurement Uncertainty

Authors withheld during double-blind review

AAAI-27 · under review

Flag only what the data can prove: interval-valued verifiers cut false speed flags from 27.7% to 1.6% and halve the mode-share distortion LLM repair introduces.

code · proofs

Abstract & key numbers

If repair loops obey the verifier, the verifier must be sound — yet real mobility data carries structural measurement uncertainty (zone-centroid geometry, 5-minute clock rounding, censored durations) that lets point-estimate checks manufacture violations the data cannot support. We construct verifiers that flag a trip only when every measurement completion consistent with the data violates the constraint, with a monotonicity guarantee that narrower measurement configurations always contain the sound flag set. The mode-share result replicates across five open-weights model families.

  • ≈ 2×extra mode-share distortion under point-estimate verification
  • 11,933trips audited in a municipal household travel survey

Auditing Verifiers, Prompts, and Transport Statistics in LLM Travel-Diary Repair

Kervin Joshua Lucas, Tengfeng Lin, Inhi Kim

Transportation Research Part C · in preparation

Passing checks ≠ recovering truth: LLMs resolved up to 86% of flags in two national travel surveys but restored the known true value in barely a third of cases.

protocol · data deposit

Abstract & key numbers

Auditing the Daejeon subset of Korea's 2021 National Travel Survey and Australia's VISTA FY2022/23, this study separates verifier compliance, truth recovery, and preservation of population transport statistics — three things usually conflated. Unsupported verifier rules, not real data errors, decided which records were sent for repair; repaired pipelines cut observed walk share by 8.4–14.4 percentage points; and evidence-qualified screening found zero cases where released fields could establish an automatic replacement. The contribution is a protocol that separates review from rewriting and evaluates population effects under the official survey design.

Verification Governance for Generative AI in Official Travel Data

Kervin Joshua Lucas, Inhi Kim

Transport Policy · VSI on Generative AI · planned

Who audits the algorithm that audits the data? A governance framework and agency-facing checklist for generative AI entering official statistics pipelines.

Scope

The policy companion to the technical arc. GenAI data tools are entering the layer that feeds mode-share statistics, demand models, and investment cases; the documented failure is sociotechnical — a "verified" label transferred institutional trust to broken output while no actor owned data-defect liability. Proposes provenance requirements, verifier auditing, soundness standards, and procurement language for transport agencies.

Chapter 6 design

Chapter 6 generalizes the repair loop into a generation loop: an LLM proposes a full-day activity schedule for a persona, a sound scheduler-verifier audits it, provable violations return as structured critiques, and the loop accepts only at zero provable violations — every accepted schedule carrying a constraint certificate. Built on the NHTS 2022 NextGen survey (10,592 person-day chains, 31,074 trips), evaluated on the Chapter 2 trilemma axes against transformer, Markov, and rule-based baselines.
